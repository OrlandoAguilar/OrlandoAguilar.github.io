In this example, I have written a interpolation for the sphere and the objects around it 
(although they have an extra shift applied from javascript).
The dual quaternions are A and B, defined as angle of rotation, axis of rotation 
and displacement in the Transformation constructor below:

DQ A=Transformation(0.0,vec3(0,1,0), vec3(-1.0,0.0,0.0));		
DQ B=Transformation(0.9,normalize(vec3(1,1,0)), vec3(1.0,0.0,0.0));				

The interpolation is performed using a oscillating time.
DQ Trf=InterpolateSlow(A,B,abs(sin(time)));
